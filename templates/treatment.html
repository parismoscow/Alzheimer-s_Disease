Current treatment options for Alzheimer's Disease primarily involve easing the symptoms. There is no cure for AD and no known way of slowing its progression. This is partly due to the difficulty of identifying the disease in its early stages.
In 2017, the United States spent $259 billion in costs related to AD. 1 in 6 caregivers of those with dementia have had to quit their jobs. The emotional and financial toll of this disease is extreme.
While there is no proven way of preventing Alzheimer's and dementia, there are many approaches which have shown some promise. These include the following:
  • Maintaining a cognitively active lifestyle by doing such things as playing chess, socializing, playing a musical instrument, and learning a 2nd language.
  • Exercising regularly.
  • Drinking red wine and caffeinated beverages.
  • Keeping a Japanese or Mediterranean diet.
